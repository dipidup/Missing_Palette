The Art Materials.

A curated list of the brushes, paints, papers, and sketchbooks I rely on every day. No paid endorsements - just honest notes on what each one is good for, with links to where I buy them.

Some links below are Amazon affiliate links - if you buy through them, I get a small commission at no extra cost to you. Helps keep the studio running.

Quick-start kits.

If you want to start without overthinking, these are the bundles I'd hand to a beginner on day one.

The watercolor starter.

If you've never painted before, start here. This is what I'd buy for myself if I had to begin again with ₹3,000.

  • A 12-pan student-grade watercolor set
  • One round brush, size 8 (any soft synthetic)
  • A small block of cotton paper, 200gsm or higher
  • A graphite pencil, kneaded eraser, water cup
See full watercolor list →

The oil painting starter.

Oils are intimidating, but cheaper than people think. This is the budget-conscious entry kit.

  • Six tubes of oil paint (titanium white, ivory black, two reds, two yellows, two blues)
  • Three flat brushes - size 4, 8, 12
  • One canvas board, 12×16 inches
  • Linseed oil + odorless turpentine
  • A palette and a palette knife
