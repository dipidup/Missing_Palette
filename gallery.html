01 - Watercolor

ढलती मुस्कान  · Dhalti Muskaan, 2018

Painted in water and light.

Watercolor has been my primary medium since 2016 - transparent, unforgiving, and alive. These works span portraits painted from life, plein-air studies from Banaras and Jharkhand, and quiet still-life observations. The sadhu with the smoke, the grandmother's laugh, three dawns on the same ghat.

02 - Oil

शागिर्द  · Shagird, 2021

Painted in depth and time.

Oil allows what watercolor doesn't - revision, layering, and a texture you can feel. These paintings are mostly figures: a tribal father, a snake charmer, a monk lost in thought, a child from Aleppo. Each one takes weeks. Each one starts with a single face I can't stop looking at.

04 - Acrylic

Confusion · Acrylic, 2018

Painted in colour and experiment.

Acrylic is my most experimental medium - fast, opaque, and forgiving of risk. These nine pieces range from abstract gesture to figurative studies: a Hercules series, mini canvases, and the chaotic energy of Confusion, painted in one sitting in 2018.

05 - Out of the box

Starry Night Sneaker · 2024

Painted on something you can wear.

A white sneaker, painted by hand - loosely inspired by Starry Night, but reworked in my own palette and brush rhythm. The idea was simple: take something meant for walls and let it move.

The surface bends, resists, interrupts the stroke. You don’t fully control it - and that’s what makes it interesting.

06 - Workshop

Teaching workshop, 2021

Taught in studios and village schools.

Since 2016, Missing Palette has run workshops in Pune, Ranchi, and rural Jharkhand - including a session for children at Mahilong village under the Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana. These photographs are from those sessions: students, canvases, and the particular quiet of a room learning to look.
