02 — About

Engineer by degree, artist by every other measure.

Based in India · Working in watercolor, oil, sketch, and acrylic

Missing Palette® is my studio practice. I'm an Indian artist working primarily in watercolor and oil — my subjects are sadhus, market vendors, grandmothers, Banaras ghats at dusk — pulled from a country I'm constantly travelling through, sketchbook in hand.

By day I'm a management consultant; by every other measure, an artist. I paint from life, mentor emerging artists, and teach Hindi to non-Hindi-speakers — mostly foreigners curious about India through its language. The disciplines feed each other: structured thinking sharpens my teaching, and painting keeps the structure honest.

I paint faces. I paint places. I paint what I see, and what I want to remember.

The name “Missing Palette” comes from a real palette of mine, ruined by paint left to dry on it.

Process

Painting from life, not photographs.

Most of the watercolors are painted on location — sometimes in 90 minutes, sometimes across two visits. The sketchbook goes everywhere. The paint has to come up wherever there's a chair, a wall, a step.

The chaos of a place becomes part of the painting. So does the heat, the noise, the people watching over the shoulder. None of that survives a photograph. All of it survives a brush.

— Painting plein-air, Banaras Ghat

06 — Commissions

A custom portrait, by hand.

Bespoke. Limited slots each year.

Commissions are accepted in limited number each year, with a focus on portraiture — in watercolor or oil, depending on the subject and the wish of the patron.

Each commission begins with a conversation. Reference photographs are welcomed but not required. Final work is signed, dated, and dispatched in archival packaging.

i.Inquiry — you describe the subject, medium, size.
ii.Quote — pricing and timeline shared within 48 hours.
iii.Half-payment confirms the slot. Painting begins.
iv.Final work delivered, framed at request.
